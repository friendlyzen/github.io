Friendly ZEN

About Friendly Zen

Friendly Zen is a simple and relaxed approach to meditation practice.

It is based on the understanding that meditation does not need to be stern, complicated, or goal-driven. We sit, we notice, we return, and we begin again.

All parts of ourselves are welcome in this practice. Our calm parts, our worried parts, our distracted parts, our kind parts, and our difficult parts all belong.

A friendly practice

In Friendly Zen, meditation is not about becoming a special kind of person. It is about becoming more fully human.

We practise with openness, honesty, kindness, and patience. We learn to meet our life as it is, rather than constantly struggling to make it into something else.

The practice is quiet, ordinary, and practical. It can be done at home, in a group, on a cushion, on a chair, or in the middle of everyday life.

What we value

Friendly Zen values simplicity, inclusivity, care, humility, joyfulness, and the natural unfolding of wisdom in ordinary life.

We do not need to push away our thoughts or feelings. We do not need to force ourselves into a perfect meditative state. We simply sit and allow things to settle in their own time.

Little by little, this practice can help us live with more clarity, compassion, and freedom.

Teacher

Jack Zenko Wicks has trained for 14 years in the Ordinary Mind Zen School, is an ordained Zen priest, and has completed the traditional Japanese Zen koan studies curriculum.

Jack has also practised extensively in the Vipassana and Insight Buddhist traditions.

Jack's focus is on training in simple and practical ways in everyday life, favouring joyfulness, openness, forgiveness, care, inclusivity, and the enjoyment of all that life has to offer.
