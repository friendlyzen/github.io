What is Friendly Zen Meditation?

The meditation we do is relaxed meditation. We are not trying to do anything special or achieve anything. We press the pause button on all of that for a while.

All parts of ourselves are welcomed in the practice. We make space for all of it.

When we meditate, our minds tend to wander off somewhere... and eventually return. This is a natural part of meditation which we can learn to use constructively.

Why we do this practice

We do this practice to live a better life. This means a more fulfilling life with less confusion, and more clarity, about who we are and the potential and the possibilities that our life offers.

We do it to develop our capacity for enjoyment and to express our love for others and the world.

We do it because we can only really be happy and peaceful when we live in accordance with our deepest values. Meditation helps us uncover this aspect of ourselves.

Just sitting

At home, make a space where you can sit comfortably. A chair or sofa or a meditation cushion or bench are all fine.

We are doing zazen (Za 坐 "sitting", Zen 禅 "meditation") which means sitting meditation, or "just sitting".

As the practice of just sitting matures, goals are dropped, and the meditation folds into a sense of presence. This is called shikantaza (Shi 只 "only", Kan 管 "attend to", Ta 打 "the act of", Za 坐 "sitting"). As the name suggests, it is an attentive and engaged practice of "just sitting".

Body and mind "just sitting" together. And all of us doing this together. This is friendly zen!

A saying

“You should meditate for 20 minutes a day. Unless you are too busy. Then you should meditate for an hour a day.” - A Zen Master

Instructions

  1. As you enter your meditation space, give yourself permission to leave your concerns at the door for a period of time. You can go back to them after.
  2. Check in with your breath and body. Take a few moments to slow down, settle in, and arrive.
  3. Just sit. Try 5 minutes. Try 20 minutes.
  4. Check in with your breath and body. Take a moment to stretch and prepare your body to stand.
  5. That’s it. Well done!
